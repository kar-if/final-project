Facts About Black Women in Coding



As of 2022, women as a whole make up about 27% of the IT field, while black women makes up only 3% of that.



Black women represent under 7% in STEM majors and are more likely to leave the major compared to other races.



In the last seven years, the representation of black professionals in the tech sector has only increased by 1%.

Important Black Women in Coding

Annie Easley

Annie Easley was one of the first African Americans to be a computer scientist for NASA. Easley developed and implemented code used for the Centaur rocket.

Valerie Thomas

Valerie Thomas is an American data scientist that invented the illusion transmitter at NASA. The illusion transmitter is still being used by scientists to this day.

Kimberly Bryant

Kimberly Bryant is an electrical engineer and founder of Black Girls Code, a non-profit organization that teaches black girls the basics of programming.
